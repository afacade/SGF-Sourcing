Pattern 01 · best on subpage headers

Splash hero

Full-width photo (60–70vh) carrying the page title in white serif. Replaces the current quiet page-hero text block. Use a different photo per page so each subpage opens with its own mood.

Pattern 02 · replaces uniform 3-up grids

Asymmetric magazine grid

One large "hero card" paired with smaller siblings. Breaks the gridded sameness of the current Product and Packing pages, gives editorial weight to whatever you want to feature this week.

Pattern 03 · cheapest, universal upgrade

Image + italic caption

Every editorial photo gets a thin italic caption underneath — date, place, what's happening. Turns a stock-image page into a documentary one. Use on Story, Brand Story, QA.

Pattern 04 · universal treatment, CSS-only

Sepia → colour on hover

Default state is warm-toned monochrome to unify mixed photo sources (Unsplash + supplier + factory). On hover the photo blooms back to full colour. Quietly elevates the whole site without changing any imagery.

Pattern 05 · sequencing, not single shots

Diptych / Triptych

Three related photos sit as one composition. Story-telling through sequence: raw → processed → packed. Strong on Product (showing the same SKU from three angles) and Story (showing a chapter as three beats).

i. Hat-shaped trim — husk still on. ii. Diamond Cut — faceted by hand. iii. 9-up carton — ready to ship.
Pattern 06 · for chaptered narratives

Polaroid tilt

Slight ±2° rotation and a hand-laid feel. Use sparingly — once per page, on the Story or Brand-Story chapter intros. Signals "this is a family business" without saying it.

Pattern 07 · for catalogues and process steps

Horizontal scroll strip

Sideways-scrolling row of 8–12 tiles. Feels like a film reel. Strong fit for the seven Packing Formats — instead of a fixed 3-up grid, the user pans through them.

↦ Drag horizontally
Pattern 08 · organic, gallery-feeling

Masonry / mosaic

Cards keep their native aspect ratio — tall, square, wide — and pack into staggered columns. Replaces the uniform 220px-tall card grid with something less catalogue, more gallery.

Pattern 09 · transformation moments

Before / After slider

Drag a handle to wipe between two states — whole green coconut → Diamond Cut, or raw harvest pile → palletised carton. Strong on Product and Sourcing pages.

Pattern 11 · for the Story page

Sticky scrollytelling

The image column stays put while the text scrolls. As each chapter passes, the image cross-fades to a new shot. Used by Stripe, Bloomberg longreads — works beautifully for the four-chapter Story.

i.

Where the river writes the soil

Before there is a coconut, there is the river. The Mekong carries silt from a thousand miles of mountain, laying it on the low fields of Ben Tre and Long An year after patient year.

ii.

The hands that know the tree

Our farmers climb by hand, because a bruised nut is a broken promise. They've done this for thirty, forty years — long enough that they read each tree the way a conductor reads a score.

iii.

Cut like a diamond

At the packing house, each husk is carved away by hand — faceted into the clean pointed form the trade calls Diamond Cut. Every nut shrink-wrapped, labelled, and logged to HACCP standards.

iv.

Farm to global

Thirty to forty-five days. That is how long it takes for an SGF container to leave Cat Lai port and arrive in Rotterdam, Los Angeles, Tokyo, or Dubai.

Pattern 12 · universal interaction layer

Lightbox on click

Tap any thumbnail → it expands to fill the screen with arrow navigation. Keeps the page light while letting visitors see the photo at full scale. Pairs with any of the grid patterns above.
