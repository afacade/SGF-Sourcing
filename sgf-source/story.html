— Born in Ben Tre · Built for the World —

A family business, written in coconut palms.

SGF Sourcing began the way most good Vietnamese businesses do — around a kitchen table, with a problem worth solving and people who had been solving it for generations.

i.

The land that taught us

Ben Tre is not just our origin — it is our curriculum.

Three rivers split the province into a delta of islands, and every island grows coconuts. The Vietnamese call it the "Land of Coconuts" for a reason: more than 70,000 hectares of palms feed a culture that drinks the water, cooks with the milk, builds furniture from the wood, and burns the husk for charcoal. Our founders grew up here. Their parents and grandparents farmed here. When they decided to build an export company, they did not have to learn the crop — they had to learn the world.

ii.

From kitchen table to container port

A coconut leaves the grove sweet. Most of them stop being sweet before they reach the buyer.

That was the problem we set out to fix. In the early years we did everything ourselves — sourcing, sorting, peeling, packing — because no one else would do it to our standards. We learned the certifications. We learned the paperwork. We learned what an importer in Rotterdam needs that an importer in Tokyo does not. Ten years later, SGF Sourcing ships to four continents, but the standard has not changed: every coconut leaves our packing house the way we would want one to arrive at our own kitchen.

iii.

A premium brand, a fair price

Premium does not have to mean expensive.

We work directly with the farms — no middleman, no auction-floor markup. That keeps the price honest. What we add on top is reliability: GlobalGAP and HACCP certification, a 60-day cold-chain shelf life, and a sales team that answers within 24 hours. Our buyers come back not because we are the cheapest, but because they never have to chase us. A premium brand, in our definition, is one that simply does what it said it would.

iv.

Connecting markets, keeping roots

Our tagline is "Vietnam Agri · Connecting Markets" — both halves matter.

Every container we ship is a small bridge between a Mekong Delta farmer and a foreign buyer. We take pride in walking that bridge in both directions: bringing Vietnamese harvests out to the world, and bringing the world's standards back into our growers' practices. That is how a country exports more than just a product — it exports a reputation. We hope to keep building ours for another fifty years.
