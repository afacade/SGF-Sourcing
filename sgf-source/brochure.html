— The Story —
i.

Where the river writes the soil

Before there is a coconut, there is the river.

The Mekong carries silt from a thousand miles of mountain and jungle, laying it on the low fields of Ben Tre and Long An year after year. The water turns brackish; the soil turns rich beyond belief. No fertilizer teaches these groves to grow sweetness — the land does it for us, at Brix 7° to 9°.

ii.

The hands that know the tree

A coconut is not a product yet when it leaves the grove.

Our farmers climb by hand, because a bruised nut is a broken promise. They have done this for thirty, forty years — long enough to read each tree the way a conductor reads a score. We do not only export coconuts. We export the dignity of the Vietnamese farmer.
